Optimizing Mangosteen Storage Life via Derivatives

Interactive simulation of post-harvest respiration rates ($\text{CO}_2$ production) using calculus.

Contextual Data Source: Inspired by MARDI (Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute) post-harvest physiological profiles for local climacteric fruits.

🌱 The Bioscience Scenario

As an Agro-Product technologist in Malaysia, managing the post-harvest shelf life of the Mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana) is critical. Mangosteen exhibits a climacteric respiratory rise after harvest.

If carbon dioxide ($\text{CO}_2$) production accelerates too fast, the fruit spoils rapidly. By tracking the exact rate of change of respiration over time using derivatives, we can pinpoint exactly when to adjust modified atmosphere packaging (MAP) or lower cold-room temperatures.

🧮 The Mathematical Model

Respiration Curve Function $f(x)$:
$f(x) = 0.05x^3 - 1.2x^2 + 8x + 20$

Where $x$ = Hours post-harvest, and $f(x)$ = $\text{CO}_2$ concentration ($\text{mg}\cdot\text{kg}^{-1}\cdot\text{h}^{-1}$).

The Derivative $f'(x)$ (Rate of Change):
$f'(x) = 0.15x^2 - 2.4x + 8$

Calculated via the Power Rule: $\frac{d}{dx}[ax^n] = n \cdot ax^{n-1}$.
